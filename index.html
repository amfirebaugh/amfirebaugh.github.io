Brief Bio:

The oldest of three girls, I grew up a little bossy, very talkative, independent, and hardworking. I was naturally motivated in school and performed well all the way through college, graduating with three majors: Physics, Math, and French. This is still one of my proudest accomplishments as a young adult. After undergrad I began a graduate program in Materials Science & Engineering, thinking that I would go towards a more practical route for a career. However, I found out quickly that I didn't enjoy it at all. I left the program and came back home. In my soul searching I decided to begin my journey to become a school teacher. I taught physics and oceanography for three years until I came to the conclusion that this too was not going to be the best fit for me, career wise, either. I knew I had to leave teaching, and with the cyclical timing of a school year combined with the timing of my decisions, I left my job at the conclusion of a school year. At this time I only had basic, initial thoughts about potential next steps in my life. Truly, I was terrified about what I would do next and how every aspect of my life would be affected. I'm a deteremined soul, though, and so it was back to the drawing board. This time around I took my soul searching a little more seriously. My heart strings kept pulling towards the idea of computer programming, and thus began my latest exciting adventure!
